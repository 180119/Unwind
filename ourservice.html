CUDDELING WITH PUPPIES


UNWIND offers the unique experience to have some quality time with cute puppies. These puppies are borrowed from a shelter and also crave the attention of students. More and more studies show that pets and pets can significantly reduce our stress. Stroking and giving attention to animals has a relaxing effect.

 

Research shows that while stroking or hugging, the amount of stress hormone (cortisol) in our blood decreases. At the same time, the so-called cuddle hormone (oxytocin) actually increases. In America, research has previously been conducted into the effect of dogs on students. At the University of Vancouver, students were allowed to play with dogs, pet and cuddle the animals. Questionnaires showed that 37% of students felt more energized after playing with the dogs and 27% said they were happier than before.

CLAYING


Are you looking for an activity where you can use your creativity and do you like to work with your hands? Then our activity claying might be for you. We at UNWIND provide you with the necessary materials so that you can get started. Be sure to give it a try, because clay has a very de-stressing effect. Do not hesitate and book a time slot right away.


 

As mentioned earlier, claying is an effective way to reduce stress. This is because your brain focuses on clay and thereby forgets the stressful thoughts. We offer all necessary materials and several types of clay. We also offer several examples and help guides that explain step by step how to get started. The nice thing about this activity is that you not only reduce your own stress through clay, but you can also give, exhibit or use your artwork to brighten up your room.

PAINTING


As a final activity, UNWIND offers the opportunity to paint. We will offer all the necessary materials, such as brushes, and paper or canvas so that you can get started. Let yourself go creatively and put aside all stressful thoughts. Not only is dyeing fun to do and it stimulates the creative processes in the brain, it also has a strong anti-stress effect.


 

Whether you could recreate a Vincent van Gough or can barely draw a stick figure, art is a fantastic way to reduce stress. Results of a 2016 study published in the Journal of the American Art Therapy Association found that just 15 minutes of creative activity can reduce your stress, regardless of artistic experience or talent. It can take your mind off things. Creating art can take your mind off of whatever is stressing you, at least for a few minutes. It's difficult to keep ruminating on your problems when you're focused on creating.
